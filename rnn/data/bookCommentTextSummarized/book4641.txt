I've heard a lot about P.
Howard aka Rejtő Jenő, the great Hungarian humorist, with his colorful characters, weird and unusual situations, and a constant attack on your laughing muscles.
So apparently I came to read this book with very high expectations, and ended up a bit disappointed.
The characters were truly eclectic, but a bit one-sided.
The situations were surreal enough, but the humor, for some reason seemed to fail me.
However, the world of fist fighters, stowaways, secret clubs of the Singapore underworld, mixed with murder on a luxury ocean liner and a plot of overthrowing a small island monarch makes an interesting mix (never mind the coherence of the story though) deserving the three stars.
