The village is full of thoroughly believable and sometimes endearing personalities, and Hamsun's fondness for human foible gets a thorough workout!
Hamsun's superb understanding of human psychology not only makes this a great pleasure to read, but also intersects so unusually with the business cycle.
Hamsun understands August very well; he describes his successes and failures, his flaws and his attributes, with great clarity but also with a certain kindness and sympathy.
If Hamsun is being rediscovered in this, his 150th anniversary year, this is a book that needs to be rescued.

Primer novela que leo del escritor noruego. Tiene un tono (lenguaje, narrativa) muy clásico: constante, sin ninguna frase o párrafo que opaque a las otras, muy bien balanceado. La historia, muy interesante, los personajes bien desarrollados...todo incorporado de una manera muy completa. No es una novela de párrafos o frases estrella, sino una obra de arte valiosa en su conjunto total. Recomendable.
