After they go off to war, and Al is severely injured in combat, he is brought in by the army to visit Birdy in a psychiatric hospital.
The army psychiatrist hopes that Al will be able to lead Birdy out of this delusional existence and back to reality.
Al talks to Birdy about their childhood exploits and eventually about his experiences in combat.
The fascinating perspectives of both the main characters reveal the author’s deep and detailed understanding of birds, as well as of humans, and also the harsh, gut-wrenching experience of horrific combat.
