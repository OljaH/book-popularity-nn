'Baltagul' is a book that, to my great surprise, has stuck with me for the past many years.
I first read it for school and then again for my own pleasure, and I remember it quite well.
This is a fascinating detective book that sets itself apart easily from the normal classic Romanian literature.
A central theme is still the need of the peasant for wealth, but it is woven into a whole new style of narrative, less common.
For the Romanian boys and girls out there, I would recommend they read this after school.

The book is about one Romanian myth: "mitul mioritic" about the values and beliefs connected to the transhumance.
The everlasting belief of human reintegrating into the universal cycle of life and bestowing the sense of belonging on the next generations.
The message is profound when we look into Romanian mythology.
It is about tradition and general valid moral values about family, forgiveness, love, perseverance, justice, faith and common sense.This myth kept the Romanians united spiritually saving them from dehumanization.
It is eventually about the communion between Human and Nature.I read this novel long ago and I'll be reading it with pleasure again, thinking simple about the simpleton and the true values of life.
