Wells was also outspoken against racism, so the import of the section is uncertain.Wells is on firmer ground with a sophisticated argument for the single transferable vote (STV).
The STV is an idea whose time has seemingly not yet come, but which is still actively discussed in the international electoral community.
The core idea is straightforward - rather than voting for one candidate or party, you mark your choices in order of preference.
Any votes above the minimum needed to win get transferred to voters' second choices.
Similarly, if your candidate can't win, your second choice gets your vote.
