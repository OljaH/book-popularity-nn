One of my favorite love stories ever.
A washed-up, middle-aged, divorced French actor named Francois meets a beautiful stranger named Kay in a Greenwich Village diner.
Simenon is a master at letting us into Francois' head and describingthe hatred and constant betrayal that come with obsessive love.
Francois reminds me of the protagonists in Alberto Moravia's BOREDOM and Stanley Kubrick's EYES WIDE SHUT.
He obsessively tries to reconstruct all of Kay's previous sexual encounters in his mind and interrogates her constantly.
You wonder if he will ever achieve peace, if he'll ever truly possess Kay. I was in tears reading the last page.
