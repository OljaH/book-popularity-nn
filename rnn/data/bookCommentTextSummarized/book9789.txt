The first half of this book is very good, with Jonas' experiences of not fitting in to normal school being an interesting story.
I've read a lot of books through the years, but no author have focused so much on noses.The only part I did not like in the first half of the book was the description of how Jonas perceived the world.
We move away from Jonas, and have to read through some quite long parts about other characters where a lot of it really is not relevant for the story.
The book follows the two and a half first years of Jonas primary education, and we are introduced to a great variety of (male) individuals who each are presented with a full background story and complexity of character.
We meet his friends, parents, uncle, the staff at two schools and their friends, and of course the helpful strangers.
Some of these could be called stereotypes, of course, but this description is more suitable for the female characters.
However, the story is still good, and, through Jonas story, we are presented with a critique of the educational sysyem.
