Like his other children's novel, The King of Ireland's Son, Colum's The Boy Who Knew What the Birds Said embeds several folktales into a single narrative.
Unlike that other book, the tales here are not woven so cleverly and intricately together to form a single cohesive story- nevertheless, The Boy Who Knew...
The book, as a whole, did not settle into my heart in the same way as The King of Ireland's Son; nonetheless it has its own distinct charms and is well worth the small amount of time it would take to read it.
