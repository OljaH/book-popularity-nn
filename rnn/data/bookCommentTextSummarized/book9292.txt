In The Cowards, Škvorecký - whose novel takes place over the course of a week and which takes into account virtually every thought the main character, Danny Smiricky, thinks and every action he does - lays out the transition from Nazi-occupied Czechoslovakia to Red Army occupied Czechoslovakia.
Škvorecký's writing style hearkens to Kerouac, at least in my opinion, as it is a kind of stream of consciousness which he uses to display the young Danny Smiricky's superficial youthful thinking (though it is unclear whether or not Škvorecký had read anything by the then-little known Kerouac, he is notable for being a fan of American literature and for later being a professor of that subject when he emigrated to Canada).
