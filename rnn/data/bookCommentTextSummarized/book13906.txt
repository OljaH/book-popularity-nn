Still, I found this book rewarding for Mahfouz's simple yet moving portrayal of a range of different personalities living through a time when both Egypt and the structure of the family are changing.
Despite all the social change, however, the extended family remains central to the majority of Mahfouz's "lives." This book, like so many of Mahfouz's works, also presents the complexity of religious (or sometimes "irreligious") sentiment found in a single family.
While the existence of very different degrees of religious devotion in a family should not surprise us, books like this gain a particular value at a time when people in Islamic countries are too easily reduced to stereotypes.
