Certainly the major element of the writing is that science (at times very strange science) is a means to get into the emotional conflicts that technology creates.
The stories roughly and subtly follow several characters literally through their lives - one born in the first story and appearing numerous times as he ages.
The "science" is really secondary, much different western Sci-Fi. One certainly won't ponder the feasibility of the future in these stories, but their emotions, the lost displaced feeling is very much something that one can ponder and relate to.
