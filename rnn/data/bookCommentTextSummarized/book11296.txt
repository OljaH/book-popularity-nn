This is the title of Elsa Morante's huge novel of 1974, which contrasts the history of the Second World War and its aftermath with the story of a poor family in Rome, so marginal as to have no chance of appearing in any history books at all.
Up to the Allied liberation of Rome, events in Italy were still being played out on the world stage, and Morante makes the irony of perspective between her hidden story and the march of history work perfectly.
