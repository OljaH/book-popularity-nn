Man and Crisis is all about the concept of the historical crisis of culture.
In it Ortega espouses his theory of the generation to historical interpretation.
The streamlined gist of the book is that cultures have a tempo.
Over the course of generations, the culture Man is born into loses less and less of the fundamental inspiration and reality it had to their forebears.
The book goes into all this with much more clarity and rigor, so if you're reading this, hopefully I've whetted your curiosity.

The whole of the drama is one's life though, one's person-hood.
To know a person, then, one must not just study acts or scenes in the play but the whole life.
Each life, too, is a part of the greater drama of human existence.
Tumult happens as the participants in the great drama adjust to the new scene, the new stage props, the new costumes and characters.
This, of course, is just as when each person adjusts to some new scene or even in one's own drama.These are some of the many ponderous and critically important thoughts that are throughout Ortega's great work.
