Another galloping story of romance, adventure, and lost-world adventure from H. Rider Haggard, best know for his novels "She" and "King Solomon's Mines." Recommended for those who enjoy a good saga of past civilizations. Haggard was inspired by the discovery of Machu Picchu and its Peruvian ruins in 1911.I edited this work for modern publication, correcting numerous errors that existed in the 1922 first edition. Haggard has a wonderful way of using antique language and phrasing to really make us feel that we're experiencing a bygone age.
In the case of "Virgin", Haggard explores love, friendship, and women, all while Adventuring across the Atlantic and over South America.Historically, Haggard does a nice job blending Inca history and myth into his adventure tale.
Bingham hadn't yet connected Machu Picchu to Pachacuti, but myth had already surrounded the Inca ruler who's credited with expanding Inca rule to cover a huge swath of territory on South America's western coast.Haggard's story unfolds as a "modern day" anitque hound translates 400-year-old letters found in an ancient chest.
While their loves develops rather abruptly, Haggard does a nice job of using a surprisingly touching romance to further Hubert and Kari's adventure.The story is enjoyable, but takes a little effort to get used to Haggard's writing style.
First published in 1922, it is a story within a story. The framing story has an antique collector buys an old “Marriage Chest” that contains items from a old tomb in Peru. A crumbling manuscript is one of the items which the owner has decipered. This manuscript, by Hubert of Hastings, becomes the main story with him as the narrator. He flees England sometime around 1400 and finds his way to Inca Peru. It is part adventure and part Victorian era-like romance.
Unmistakable Rider Haggard, full of the sounds of conflict, personal loss, victorian christian morals and English patriotism. The pace is faster and the details are less elaborate. It flows slightly faster than some of his other books and the end is almost disappointing.
