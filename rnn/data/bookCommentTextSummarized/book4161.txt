We may have heard of Tolstoy, but not the others, I am sure, were it not for the 'propaganda' literature distributed far and wide by the Russians.Among the books scattered around my house, I found this book about two naughty little boys who throw away a telegram by their father.
The book remained in my mind, a sweet story about two energetic young children and their young mother.
They have some adventures too which could turn very serious.It is a beautifully written book and something all children should read.

Chuck and Geck are two little boys who live with their mother in Moscow, while their father works far away, deep in the taiga.
One winter, when a letter arrives from Father to say that there's too much work to allow him to come to Moscow to visit them, the two brothers are very disappointed—until they are told the second part of the missive: that Chuck, Geck and Mother should go to Father instead.
And this sets the three of them—especially Chuck (who collects every little bit of possibly handy thing that comes his way, from tin foil to metal springs) and Geck (who sings really well)—off on a fine adventure, as they tackle the taiga, with its diverse and daunting mysteries.
