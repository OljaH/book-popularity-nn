In this volume Karl Oscar begins in earnest his new life in the wilderness of territorial Minnesota.
The book begins with the family's arrival in the New York of 1850 and the long and fairly arduous journey to Chisago Lake.
The triumph Karl Oscar feels when he pushes his stick into the ground and finds NO ROCKS is quite palpable.
(If you ever visit Kronoberg in Sweden and see the heart-breaking fields full of huge erratics pushed to one side to enable a bit of planting between them, you'll understand.) The book ends with their first anniversary away from Sweden and so deals with the ambivalent feelings of the characters and shows how America is changing them.

I cannot rave enough about The Emigrant Series, by Vilhelm Moberg.
Unto a Good Land is book 2 of 4.
Moberg is a Swedish writer who published the first volume in 1949; the others quickly followed.
They chronicle a group of Swedes immigrating to the U.S.
(Minnesota).
I find the narrative storyline to be compelling - I always want to know what will happen next with this cast of characters struggling to better their lives.
I find the writing to be reminiscent of Steinbeck - lyrically descriptive, often poetic, and deeply moving.I'm so glad there are four volumes and I'm not yet halfway through them, because these books are bringing me great pleasure.
I love these books of the Swedish immigrants settling Minnesota.
In this volume, after a four month voyage across the Atlantic, the group lands in NYC and steam engines their way to Stillwater, Minnesota.
From here, they strike out on foot to stake their claims on lands still inhabited by the Chippewa and Sioux tribes.
Lake country that looks like their homeland with soil free of stones and rich, top soil up to two shovel full down.I've made it sound quite easy, but the hardship endured by these brave and adventurous souls seeking a better life is well conveyed.
I will savor the next two volumes as I read them throughout the coming year.
