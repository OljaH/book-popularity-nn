Sounding very much like Thomas Aquinas, he explains how the horizontal line of the cross represents the physical world of constant change and duality- like a time line- and how the vertical line is the Primordial state of being: the static, eternal Now through which the manifested world flows out of.
A thing I'm not qualified to judge is his apparent knowledge of mathematics, which he uses to discuse the many dimensions and modalities of different states of being and how they all issue forth from the horizontal cross- I'll admit it got over my head at times.
What's important is not necessarily the information, but the number of comparisons made between all these seemingly different religions and philosophies.
Truly changes the way that you look at perpendicular, intersecting lines
