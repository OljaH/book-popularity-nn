When one picks up a Dan Brown book there are certain expectations.
One expects that religion or religious institutions will play a central role in the story.
One can expect that there will be puzzles to be solved and mysteries within mysteries.
One can expect that there will be a considerable quantity of payload in the form of interesting, arcane information.
One can expect that once begun it will be a difficult book to put down.
And Brown delivers on all of the above.
And there are some notions at the end of the book that may be a bit much to swallow.
He truly believes he has been invested with the power of 'omniscience', and he looks down at the reader as if he were talking to a bunch of retarded individuals (which we actually probably are just by virtue of the fact that we are reading his books).
