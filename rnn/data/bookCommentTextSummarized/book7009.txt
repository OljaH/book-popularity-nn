Astrid Lindgren flawlessly knows what interests children.And that is certainly true of Superdetective Blomkvist: an exciting story about three children who go on an adventure in their village, they fish, they climb trees, they fight with a rival gang (which actually consists of their friends, but well, you have to keep it interesting).
It's not the most well-known book by Astrid Lindgren, but it has all the essential qualities of her fiction - the idyllic surroundings and descriptions of a happy childhood.
He is very interested in detective work and he solves three mysteries in the three books Lindgren wrote about him (the others are called Bill Bergson Lives Dangerously and Bill Bergson and the White Rose Rescue).
Kalle has two friends, Eva-Lotta and Anders, with whom he spends his summer, playing a game called The War of the Roses, and who also help him solve the crimes, even though they aren't too enthusiastic about Kalle's keen interest at first.
Teenager Kalle Blomkvist (Bill Bergson in English) spends the summer days playing with his friends Eva-Lotta and Anders, and once in a while they team up to fight the rival “gang” over real or invented disagreements.
When uncle Einar, a distant relative of Eva-Lotta shows up, Kalle eyes a possibility to get his breakthrough.
If only uncle Einar could do something suspicious...The story about Kalle is about the inventiveness and imagination that emerges from good old boredom.
Combine stories like this with some basic offline time, and you’ve (read: I’ve) got yourself (myself) a massive stress reducer.
