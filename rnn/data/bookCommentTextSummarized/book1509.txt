first time I've read Pirandello, who I think is best known for his drama. (this is a novel.) It's a complex book, much more so than I thought upon reading the first couple of chapters. I recommend making a diagram or chart of the characters to aid in keeping track of them. If you read it. The narrator is a camera operator for an Italian film studio. The book was published in 1916, when cinema was a relatively new art form. The narrator simply cranks the camera and observes, as he says repeatedly, "impassively." An strangely & subtly unsettling book.
The main character is a cameraman, basically turning the wheels to send the film through in budding cinecitta. All about the mechanization of human life, and all kinds of superficialities, into which he also gets dragged by friends and company. Also one of the most passive characters I've ever come across. A few interesting themes which underpin the whole of the story, and a very well and consistently wrought book. Definitely not as original as "Sei personnaggi..."
