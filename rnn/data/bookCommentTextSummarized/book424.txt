Clason published a series of pamphlets beginning in 1926 with financial advice couched in Babylonian parables, which were later combined in book form and given the title, The Richest Man in Babylon.
Our financial advisor gave us two copies of this slim book for our daughters and their husbands and I slipped them into their Christmas stockings this past year.
One read it and is following the advice closely, crediting it for their improved financial situation, while the other hasn't yet bothered to read it.
