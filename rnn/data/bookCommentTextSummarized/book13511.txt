The Road to Los Angles by John Fante introduces one of the most bizarre, disturbed, and likeable alter egos in literature, Arturo Bandini.
The book takes place in 1930’s Los Angles, primarily the rough neighborhoods around the harbor docks.
The characters are fleshed out, but what’s truly interesting is how Fante instills personality into inanimate objects that come to life in Bandini’s warped mind.
Fante really captures the essence of the grimy, foggy, streets of Los Angles and the blue collar lifestyle.
The Road to Los Angles is a book that swings hard with crude impact and special brand of finesse.
