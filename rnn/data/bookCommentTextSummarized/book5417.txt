Many conversations of great length between a teen-aged admirer of Kafka, - who was a colleague and I think a subordinate of Janouch's father at the insurance company he worked for.
The conversations are completely believable, often edifying, inspiring, touching - and yet it's hard to believe that they ever really took place.
Still, the glimpses of Kafka the man, the care he takes with the development of the quite immature boy over the years in which this friendship took place, and the flashes of the real Kafka is hard to describe yet creates an appetite to relive it.
