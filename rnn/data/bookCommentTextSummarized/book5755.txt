A World Apart is an informative, descriptive and often harrowing account of life inside a Soviet prison camp during the early 1940s.
A lot of the book details how the camps worked, treatment of different racial groups, nationalities (the author is Polish) and genders by both the Soviet guards and also the prisoners and stories about how he and his fellow prisoners came to be in the situation that they found themselves in.
He also discusses the human spirit and how people react differently to such horrible conditions and treatment.I would highly recommend this book to anyone who has an interest in modern history and/or the history of the Soviet Union.
