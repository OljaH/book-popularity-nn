Book Set against the backdrop of a crumbling matrilineal ''tarawad'' system of the Nairs in Kerala with its petty rivalries and jealousies, its sambandhan system of contractual marriage, its conflicts and problems, and the changes in land legislation which sharply erode the feudal order.
Kaalam is the story of sethumadhavan Nair who starts out as an ambitious and confident adolescent - but in his journey towards adulthood, where material and social success go hand in hand, he is faced with an overwhelming sense of disillusionment.
In a finesse characteristic of Nair's profound, yet minimalist sense of expression, the starkness of reality found in this story is touching and insightful.
But being in El Salvador, where let alone Malayalam books, but also English ones being scarce to come by, I had to settle for the eBook version.
He matures and falters and makes mistakes and drifts through life as do the others around him like his first crush, his family, his first love, his employers and later his wife.
Like the river in his village, ebbing and flowing, sluggish at times and fast-flowing at others, Sethu meanders through his life not really achieving much and wondering whether the worst is yet to come or already behind him.A short read rooted in stark reality.
I"d recommend reading it in Malayalam though, to enjoy the book better.
