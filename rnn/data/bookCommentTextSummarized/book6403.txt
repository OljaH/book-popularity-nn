The Skin is a novel whose narrative runs the course of the Italian campaign of World War II, from the Salerno landings to peace.
And it's a novel drenched in symbolism and satire which more than bites, it devours.Early on Curzio Malaparte writes about Americans: "They do not know although they are in many respects the most Christian nation in the world, that without evil there can be no Christ."
