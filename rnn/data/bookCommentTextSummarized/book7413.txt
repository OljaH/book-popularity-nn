Japrisot falls to the mystery novel trap of building up an immensely intriguing and suspenseful narrative that he has to then tear apart clumsily in the last 30 pages to explain himself.
The first two "acts" are stunning psychological noir material, fast-paced and full of character intricacies, secrets, and neuroses; an absolute thrill to read, the kind that forces your hand to keep turning the pages.
However, the final act is a gaping flaw in every aspect from character to suspense to plausibility to atmosphere and so on.
I'd like to read more of his work if I can find translations somewhere in this city....
He creates intricate plots with complex characters and the story moves in a way that you just don't want to stop reading.
This is probably my all time favorite book of his (although I have yet to read them all--I'm working my way through them).What I love most about this book is that you are constantly questioning whether Dany is telling the truth or if she is crazy.
It's done so well that you want to keep reading so that you find out the truth.
Japrisot weaves such a great plot that's equal parts mystery and psychological thriller that you are sucked into the book and don't want to leave.
