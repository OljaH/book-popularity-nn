Somewhere halfway between Dennis the Menace and William, Nicholas is a regular French schoolboy who gets into various scrapes at school and home. With its schoolyard politics, tantrums, visiting grannies and whatnot these tales feel like snapshots of a kind of childhood that felt universal when I was a child myself but is already markedly different from what most urban children today experience. These are not very deep stories, they're more like pleasantly entertaining squibs, and a great way to while away a lazy afternoon.

Nicholas in Trouble is a funny little book by the French author Rene Goscinny.
My favourite story in this book is called 'Roulette', in which one of the boys at Nicholas's school brings in a roulette wheel and all the boys start playing with it.
Like most of the stories involving Nicholas's friends, it ends in a punch-up between most of the boys.
The series was written over 50 years ago but it could have been written even today.
Although a little dated at times (Nicholas says his family don't have a television yet), kids today will enjoy it as much as people did when it was first published.
Il dit oui avec le cœurÉcrites sous forme de courts récits autobiographiques, les aventures du Petit Nicolas s'alternent et s’étalent sur plusieurs recueils, mettant en scène un petit et très attachant garçon dans un environnement urbain pendant les années 1960. Les thèmes de l'enfance (la camaraderie, les disputes, les rapports avec la maîtresse d'école… ) mais également le monde complexe des adultes (l'éducation, les disputes familiales… ) sont abordés par Goscinny avec l’humour et la tendresse propres à l’age du protagoniste, et se libèrent de la page écrite grâce aux dessins très expressifs de Sempé, qui enrichissent les publications.
