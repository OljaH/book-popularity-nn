Excellent action-adventure-thriller! This is the third book by James Rollins that I've read, and I think I've enjoyed each one a little bit more than the last. This one is filled with continuous action with great science mystery on top. I will definitely be continuing with the series as well as checking some of Mr. Rollins other books as well.
Nazi biological experiments and Himmler's fascination with the Occult are historical fact, though they have proven ripe for fictional conspiracy theories (and no one seems to feel too bad about playing around with the reality of their atrocities...) I can't speak for how accurately Rollins explained quantum mechanics, but as usual, I was able to follow it and it seemed to gel with what little I have learned about it.
Where Rollins takes that theory is of course, fictional.But I think where he went with the ideas was the best part of the book: imagining what new thoughts in science could mean for humanity, much like you'd find in a good sci-fi novel (not that I'd classify Black Order as sci-fi.) Rollins contrived a truly beautiful conclusion to the mystery.
