He and his contemporaries wrote realistic fiction before modernism with its tortured dreams and weird fantasies took hold of Kerala.Patthummayude Aadu ("Patthumma's Goat") is a fictionalised account of Basheer's family life - only thing is, as with most of his stories, we cannot say what is truth and what is fiction.
The goat belongs to Basheer's sister Patthumma (Fatima), and is characterised by a voracious appetite.
And when it starts eating his blanket after polishing off two of his novels, Basheer says: "Dear Goat, please eat all the novels you want, I can get another copy printed: but leave this blanket alone!

The story starts off with the author coming back home, the house, which was built next to the family house has been given on rent by his youngest brother, so the author has to live in his family home, along with his mother, 3 brothers and their family, chickens and cats.
