In A Very Easy Death, Simone De Beauvoir said, “She (her mother) had a very easy death; an upper class death.” But it wasn’t an easy death.
In this frank account of her mother’s struggle with intestinal cancer, Beauvoir not only reveals the struggle to release our loved ones but also the lies that we sometime perpetrate to spare them of suffering.
The process of dying was gruesome, even for her mother, who wanted to keep a stiff upper lip.
For many, like Beauvoir’s mother, dying may be a far worse ordeal than death.

In A Very Easy Death, Simone De Beauvoir said, “She (her mother) had a very easy death; an upper class death.” But it wasn’t an easy death.
In this frank account of her mother’s struggle with intestinal cancer, Beauvoir not only reveals the struggle to release our loved ones but also the lies that we sometime perpetrate to spare them of suffering.
The process of dying was gruesome, even for her mother, who wanted to keep a stiff upper lip.
For many, like Beauvoir’s mother, dying may be a far worse ordeal than death.
